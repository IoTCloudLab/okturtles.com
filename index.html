Security Theatre

We love the Electronic Frontier Foundation (EFF).

That's why we could not believe our eyes when we saw their Encrypt the Web Report, where they appear to grant various big-name companies a green stamp of approval for broken security that they tore apart only two years prior.

Does adding forward secrecy improve the security of a connection if it has been hijacked? Not in the slighest. How about "encrypting data center links"? Nope, that do not help even if the connection is secure. A rouge employee, a crafty hacker, or a simple subpoena instantly destroys every shred of security the data has. Do any of these columns help? Not a single one.

Even more astonishing is the fact that the EFF appears to be ignoring software that actually provides superior security, choosing instead to promote the empty and misleading gestures of megacorps, without so much as a footnote or disclaimer.
